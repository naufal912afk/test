Hi there,this is "REALITY CLUB" and this is how the story goes

Formation and Early Years:

Reality Club started as a collaboration between three young musicians with a shared love for music: Fathia Izzati (vocals), Era Patigo (drums), and Mayo (bass). Fathia, who had built a following as a YouTuber known for covering popular songs (including "City of Stars" from La La Land), was encouraged by Mayo to join forces and form a band.

However, the trio felt they needed more members to enhance their sound. Fathia then invited her brother, Faiz Novascotia Saripudin (guitar and vocals), and her boyfriend, Iqbal Angga Kusumah (guitar), to complete the group. Thus, the Reality Club as we know it was formed. After six months, Nugi Wicaksono replaced Mayo on bass, solidifying the band's lineup.

Homemade Beginnings:

The band’s early days were marked by a DIY spirit. They wrote songs in Faiz’s room, and to give their tracks a professional sound on a budget, they recorded parts of their debut album in a closet. This indie, homegrown approach gave their music a raw, authentic edge, which resonated with fans. Their first releases on SoundCloud introduced listeners to their fresh sound, with "Okay" being the first track that caught attention.

Breakthrough with Never Get Better:

Reality Club's debut album, "Never Get Better", was released on August 19, 2017. It brought a new energy to the Indonesian music scene with its vibrant indie rock sound. The album's themes revolved around the struggles of making dreams come true, touching on personal battles, insecurities, and self-doubt.

What Do You Really Know?:

Released on August 30, 2019, Reality Club's second album, "What Do You Really Know?", marked a shift in tone. The dark cover and introspective lyrics explored existential questions about life, relationships, and personal growth.

Reality Club Presents:

Their latest album, "Reality Club Presents", was released on May 25, 2023. The cinematic cover art hints at the storytelling nature of the album, which is an anthology of romantic stories. Each song tells a unique love story, from the excitement of new love to the pain of unrequited feelings.

Tours and Growth:

Reality Club’s music has earned them international recognition. They’ve toured across Asia, and in March 2024, they are set to expand their reach even further with performances in cities like Seattle, San Francisco, Los Angeles, and others.
